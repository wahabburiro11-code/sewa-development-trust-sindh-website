About Sewa Development Trust Sindh

Our Mission & Vision

Founded in 2003, Sewa Development Trust Sindh is a not-for-profit working to uplift vulnerable communities across Sindh. It holds Special Consultative Status with UN-ECOSOC since 2014. Our mission is to create a society with prosperity and secure life through advocacy, research and development.

Take a Child to School

×

This initiative focuses on increasing school enrollment and retention rates in rural Sindh through community mobilization and support. We work with local communities to identify barriers to education and develop solutions that work for them.

Our program includes:

  • Community awareness campaigns on the importance of education
  • Support for school infrastructure improvements
  • Provision of educational materials and resources
  • Teacher training programs
  • Scholarship programs for deserving students

Since its inception, this project has helped over 15,000 children gain access to quality education in rural Sindh. We've established 35 community learning centers and provided scholarships to 500+ deserving students who otherwise wouldn't have been able to afford schooling.

Our approach involves working closely with parents, community leaders, and local government officials to create sustainable education systems that will continue to benefit children for years to come.

Women Rights and Labour Laws

×

This project focuses on empowering women workers by enhancing their knowledge of labor rights and organizing them into groups for collective action. We believe that economic empowerment is key to gender equality.

Our activities include:

  • Workshops on labor laws and workers' rights
  • Legal aid and support for women facing workplace discrimination
  • Formation and support of women workers' associations
  • Advocacy for better working conditions and equal pay
  • Skills training programs for economic empowerment

To date, we have trained over 5,000 women on their labor rights and helped form 45 women workers' associations across Sindh. These associations have successfully negotiated better working conditions and fair wages for their members.

We also provide legal support to women facing discrimination or harassment in the workplace, and have helped secure justice for over 200 cases in the past three years.

End Child Early and Forced Marriages

×

This project works to eliminate the harmful practices of child early and forced marriages (CEFM) in rural Sindh through community engagement, awareness raising, and advocacy.

Our comprehensive approach includes:

  • Community dialogues and awareness sessions on the negative impacts of CEFM
  • Engagement with religious and community leaders to change social norms
  • Establishment of village protection committees to identify and prevent CEFM cases
  • Educational support for at-risk girls
  • Legal assistance for victims and their families
  • Advocacy for stronger enforcement of existing laws

Since the project began, we've prevented over 200 child marriages and helped reintegrate 150+ girls back into schools. We've trained 500 community volunteers who serve as advocates against CEFM in their villages.

Our work has contributed to a 40% reduction in reported CEFM cases in the districts where we operate, and we continue to work toward complete elimination of this harmful practice.

Shelter Construction

×

This project provides safe and durable shelter solutions for vulnerable communities affected by natural disasters in Sindh. We focus on building disaster-resilient homes that can withstand future climate challenges.

Our shelter program includes:

  • Construction of one-room shelters for families who lost their homes in disasters
  • Training community members in disaster-resilient construction techniques
  • Using locally-sourced, sustainable materials where possible
  • Incorporating designs that are culturally appropriate and climate-responsive
  • Working with communities to identify the most vulnerable families

To date, we have constructed over 2,930 shelters, providing safe housing for more than 20,000 people across Sindh. Our shelters are designed to withstand winds of up to 150 km/h and are elevated to protect against flooding.

Beyond just building shelters, we empower communities by training local builders in disaster-resilient techniques, creating a lasting skills base that continues to benefit the community long after our direct involvement ends.

Climate Leadership

×

This initiative trains youth leaders to combat climate change challenges through local adaptation plans and community-based initiatives. We believe that young people are crucial agents of change in addressing environmental issues.

Our program components include:

  • Climate change education and awareness workshops
  • Leadership training for youth environmental advocates
  • Support for community-based climate adaptation projects
  • Tree plantation drives and reforestation efforts
  • Promotion of sustainable agricultural practices
  • Advocacy for environmental policies at local and regional levels

We have trained over 500 youth climate leaders who have gone on to implement 75+ community projects addressing environmental challenges. These include water conservation initiatives, waste management programs, and sustainable farming projects.

Our climate leaders have collectively planted over 50,000 trees in deforested areas and have successfully advocated for stronger environmental protections in their local communities.

Active Citizens Program

×

This program develops leadership skills in young people to facilitate community and political participation, empowering them to become active agents of positive change in their societies.

Key elements of the program include:

  • Civic education and democratic process training
  • Leadership development workshops
  • Community project design and implementation support
  • Mentorship from established community leaders
  • Networking opportunities with like-minded youth
  • Advocacy and public speaking training

Since its launch, the Active Citizens Program has trained over 1,200 young leaders who have gone on to implement 180+ community projects addressing issues from education to environmental conservation.

Our alumni have taken on leadership roles in their communities, with many being elected to local government positions or starting their own social initiatives. The program has created a vibrant network of young change-makers who continue to support each other's efforts long after completing the training.
